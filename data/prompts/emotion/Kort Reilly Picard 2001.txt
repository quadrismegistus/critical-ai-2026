Six emotion axes for learning affect (Kort, Reilly, Picard, 2001).

Axis-state categories:
- Anxiety-Worry-Discomfort-Comfort-Hopeful-Confident
- Ennui-Boredom-Indifference-Interest-Curiosity-Intrigue
- Frustration-Puzzlement-Confusion-Insight-Enlightenment-Epiphany
- Dispirited-Disappointed-Dissatisfied-Satisfied-Thrilled-Enthusiastic
- Terror-Dread-Apprehension-Calm-Anticipatory-Excited
- Humiliated-Embarrassed-Self-conscious-Pleased-Satisfied-Proud

Context:
- Useful for transitions in emotional states during learning.

Examples:
1) "I was confused, then suddenly everything clicked." -> Frustration-Puzzlement-Confusion-Insight-Enlightenment-Epiphany
2) "I'm deeply uneasy and keep catastrophizing." -> Anxiety-Worry-Discomfort-Comfort-Hopeful-Confident
3) "I'm thrilled and highly motivated now." -> Dispirited-Disappointed-Dissatisfied-Satisfied-Thrilled-Enthusiastic
